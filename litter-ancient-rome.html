6. what is art of ancient Rome? explain.​


Answer:

The art of ancient Rome includes architecture paintings, sculpture, and mosaic work.

Ancient Roman art is a very broad topic, spanning almost 1,000 years and three continents, from Europe into Africa and Asia. The first Roman art can be dated back to 509 B.C.E., with the legendary founding of the Roman Republic, and lasted until 330 C.E. (or much longer, if you include Byzantine art). Roman art also encompasses a broad spectrum of media including marble, painting, mosaic, gems, silver and bronze work, and terracottas, just to name a few.

15. Explain the political system of Ancient Rome.


Answer:

In Roman society, the aristocrats were known as patricians. The highest positions in the government were held by two consuls, or leaders, who ruled the Roman Republic. A senate composed of patricians elected these consuls. At this time, lower-class citizens, or plebeians, had virtually no say in the government.

19. what was happening in ancient Rome at the time of ceaser.


Answer:

At the time of Julius Caesar, ancient Rome was a powerful and expanding empire. Caesar was a prominent politician and military general, known for his conquests in Gaul (modern-day France) and his leadership in the Roman Republic. However, his growing influence and popularity among the people of Rome threatened the power of the Senate, which ultimately led to his assassination in 44 BCE. Rome was undergoing significant political and social changes, with the collapse of the Republic and the rise of the Roman Empire under Augustus Caesar. The city was also undergoing urbanization and expansion, with new public buildings, roads, and aqueducts being constructed.


20. what was happening in ancient Rome at the time of Caesar.


Answer:

Julius Caesar was a well-known figure in ancient Rome during the Late Roman Republic, which lasted from 133 BC to 27 BC. Rome was a powerful and expanding empire at the time, but it also faced significant political and social unrest.

Caesar rose to prominence as a successful military commander and politician in the late 60s BC, and he became a key figure in the ongoing power struggle between Rome's ruling elite. He formed the First Triumvirate with Pompey and Crassus, which allowed him to gain significant influence and power in Rome.

During this period, Rome was experiencing economic and social upheaval, with the rich-poor divide widening and lower-class unrest increasing. Rome was also involved in a number of military campaigns, including wars with Gaul, Britain, and other neighboring states.

Caesar famously crossed the Rubicon River with his army in 49 BC, defying the Roman Senate and igniting a civil war that eventually led to his rise to power as dictator of Rome. He instituted a number of political and social reforms, including the Julian calendar and measures to alleviate poverty and improve Roman citizens' rights.

Despite his reforms, Caesar was met with strong opposition from those who saw him as a threat to the traditional Roman system of government, and he was assassinated in 44 BC by a group of senators led by Brutus and Cassius. His death signaled the start of the end of the Roman Republic, as power struggles and civil wars roiled the empire.
