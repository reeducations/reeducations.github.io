5. plot a graph with the pressure at the y axis and volume at the x axis


Boyle's Law

An experimental gas law which states the relationship  between pressure and volume is inversely proportional when the temperature and amount of gas are constant. When volume increases, the pressure decreases and when pressure increases, volume decreases.

When plotting the coordinates with gas law, initial pressure should be paired with initial volume and final volume is with final pressure. The result of the graph is going to be a straight line going downward since pressure is inversely proportional with volume.

Here is  A sample problem of gas law:

The pressure on a 425 mL sample of gas is decreased from 855mm Hg to 759 mm Hg.what will the new volume of gas be (at constant temperature)

Given Data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 425 ml                      P₁ (initial pressure) = 855 mmHg

V₂ (final volume) = ? unknown                 P ₂ (final pressure) = 759 mmHg

Solution:

We can use the formula of Boyle's Law   (P₁V₁  = P₂V₂)

                                               P₁V₁  = P₂V₂

                   855 mmHg × 425 ml  = 759 mmHg × V₂

363,375 mmHg.ml /  759 mmHg   = V₂

                                        478.75 ml = V₂

Answer:

the new volume of gas be (at constant temperature) = 478.75 ml
