10. To what extent is Philippine Art derivative of Western Art?


Philippine art has been heavily influenced by Western Art, thanks to years of colonization.

A few examples, I suggest you look up images of these artist's work to get an idea of what i'm talking about.

Juan Luna  studied in Europe and painted in a very western academic style, using realistic anatomy and perspective, as well as techniques like chiaroscuro, which was introduced by the Italian master, Carravaggio. Even the subject matter of his paintings was very western, as he painted grand scenes such as Spoliarium and The Death of Cleopatra, which are reminiscent of Romantic paintings.

Fernando Amersolo's works also show western influence, note the realistic rendering of the scenes. His works can also be compared to several European paintings of idealized rural life.

The influence of cubism can be seen in more modern filipino art, like in the work of Anita Magsaysay Ho.
