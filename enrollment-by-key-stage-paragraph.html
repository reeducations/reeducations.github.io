2. enrollment by key stage paragraph​


Answer:

Paragraphing

Students in Stage 1 use their knowledge of text features to structure longer written texts. In order to support the logical organisation of information students need to be able to use paragraphs to group like ideas and separate longer texts into appropriate segments.

Strategy

Explicit teaching

Students need to understand that sentences focussed on one idea or like ideas can be grouped together within a longer text to form paragraphs.

Paragraphs break up large blocks of text (visually as well as for meaning) so that the reader can easily follow and digest the information or ideas contained in persuasive, informative and imaginative texts.

At this stage students should learn to use paragraphs to group sentences about like ideas; separate different ideas; or, segment distinct parts of the text.

General strategies

Name, identify and explain the purpose of paragraphs in texts. Embed the terminology in your teaching to familiarise students with the paragraph as a text feature. For example, instead of asking ‘Who would like to read next?’ say ‘I read the first paragraph and I would like someone else to read the next paragraph.’

When reading shared texts in any learning areas or during guided reading, draw students attention to the use of paragraphs. Where possible prompt students before reading so that they can focus on that aspect during the reading.

For example - The text on this page is organised in three paragraphs. As I read it, I want you to think about why the writing has been organised that way. I will ask you for your ideas after I have read it.

Explicitly teach students to plan and compose texts in paragraphs with a range of graphic organisers or text scaffolds appropriate to the learning area and subject matter.

Engage students with frequent experiences of reading and viewing texts organised with a range of text features. Invite students to comment on the organisation of text and ideas. Prompt students to consider what sentences can be grouped together and what happens if that grouping is changed.

12. Writing Activity (Group) Specific Definition: Write a three- paragraph essay explaining the strand and track you are enrolled in. Emphasize the definition of important terms. Strand: TVL Track: Agriculture


Explanation:

The TVL (Technical Vocational Livelihood) strand in Agriculture is a senior high school program designed for students who are interested in pursuing a career in the agricultural industry. This strand provides students with practical skills and knowledge in the areas of crop production, animal husbandry, fisheries, and agribusiness management. The program is intended to prepare students for employment opportunities in agriculture or for further studies in college.

One of the important terms in the TVL (Agriculture) strand is agribusiness. Agribusiness is a term that refers to the business of agricultural production and the marketing of agricultural products. It involves a range of activities, including crop production, animal husbandry, processing, distribution, and marketing. Agribusiness management is an essential component of the TVL (Agriculture) strand, and students are taught how to apply business principles and practices to the agricultural sector.

Another important term in the TVL (Agriculture) strand is sustainable agriculture. Sustainable agriculture is an approach to farming that focuses on long-term environmental and economic sustainability. It involves practices that minimize the use of synthetic inputs, such as fertilizers and pesticides, and promote soil health, biodiversity, and ecosystem services. Students in the TVL (Agriculture) strand learn about sustainable agriculture practices and how to apply them in real-world farming situations.

In conclusion, the TVL (Agriculture) strand is a valuable senior high school program that provides students with practical skills and knowledge in the agricultural industry. It emphasizes the importance of agribusiness management and sustainable agriculture practices, which are essential for the long-term viability of the agricultural sector. By enrolling in this strand, students can prepare themselves for a career in agriculture or further studies in college.
