3. A 100 ml aqueous solution is labeled to contain 20% hydrochloric acid(HCl). How many grams of pure HCl are contained in the solution?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{volume of solution} = \text{100 mL} \\ & \text{percent by volume} = 20\% \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the volume of solute (HCl).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of solute} & = \frac{\text{percent by volume}}{100\%} \times \text{volume of solution} \\ & = \frac{20\%}{100\%} \times \text{100 mL} \\ & = \boxed{\text{20 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 20 mL of pure HCl in the solution.

7. What volume of 12.0 M HCl is required to make 75.0 mL of 3.50 M HCl? a) 21.9 mL b) 0.560 mL c) 257 mL d) 75.0 mL


Answer:

From the choices, the answer is letter A, 21.9 mL.

Explanation:

This problem is an application of the dilution equation in chemistry.

Dilution

Dilution is defined as the process of lowering the concentration of a solute in solution. This process can simply be done by mixing the solution with more solvent.

Solute is the substance in a solution that is being dissolved, while the solvent in a solution is the substance that is used to dissolve the solute.

The dilution equation of solutions is as follows:

C₁V₁ = C₂V₂

where

C₁ is the initial concentration of the solutionV₁ is the initial volume of the solutionC₂ is the final concentration of the solutionV₂ is the final volume of the solutionMolarity

The usual unit of the concentration of a solution is molarity. It is defined to be the moles of solute per liters of solution. In mathematical variable form, molarity is M = [tex]\frac{n}{L}[/tex].

Solving for the problem using the dilution equation, we have,

C₁V₁ = C₂V₂C₁ = 12.0 MC₂ = 3.50 MV₂ = 75.0 mL

V₁ = [tex]\frac{(C2)*(V2)}{C1}[/tex]V₁ = [tex]\frac{(3.50 M)*(75.0 mL)}{12.0 M}[/tex]V₁ = 21.875 mLV₁ = 21.9 mL (following scientific notation, round to the nearest tenths)

Therefore, the volume of the 12.0 M HCl required is 21.9 mL.

18. What is the Molarity of concentrated HCl if the solution contains 37.0% HCl by mass and if the density of the solution is 1.18 g/ml?


Answer:

The density — 1.19 g/mL — tells us that one mL of the solution has a mass of 1.19 gms. Therefore 1000 mL of the solution has a mass of 1190 grams.

Now that we know the mass of the solution we can determine the mass of the HCl in a liter. In 1190 gms of solution, 37.5 % is HCl. 1190 x 0.375 = 446.25

So, we have 446.25 gms HCl in a liter of solution. How many moles HCl do we have? The molecular weight of HCl is 36.5. Therefore, we have 446.25 / 36.5 = 12.23 moles HCl in each liter of the concentrated acid.

The molarity of the solution is 12.23. And, since HCl is a monobasic acid, the normality will also be 12.23.

24. What is the percentage composition of a 750 mL solution containing 9.0 mL HCl?​


Percent by VolumeDiscussion:

Percent by volume is the ratio of the volume of a solute to the volume of the solution, multiplied by 100. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} × 100}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} × 100}[/tex]

The percent by volume is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

27. From the following data, find the percentage purity of a sample of KHSO4: Sample = 1.2118 g NaOH used = 26.28 ml HCl used = 1.53 ml 1 ml HCl≈ 1.206 ml NaOH 1 ml HCl ≈0.02198 g Na2CO3​


Answer:

First, we need to determine the number of moles of NaOH used:

1 ml of NaOH = 1.206 ml of HCl

26.28 ml of HCl used = 31.7 ml of NaOH used (26.28 mL × 1.206 mL NaOH/mL HCl)

The number of moles of NaOH used can be calculated as:

moles of NaOH = (concentration of NaOH) × (volume of NaOH used in L)

= 0.1 M × 0.0317 L

= 0.00317 moles

Since the reaction between NaOH and KHSO4 is a 1:1 ratio, the number of moles of KHSO4 present in the sample is also 0.00317 moles.

Next, we need to determine the mass of impure KHSO4 in the sample:

1 ml of HCl ≈ 0.02198 g of Na2CO3

1.53 ml of HCl used ≈ 0.0337 g of Na2CO3 (1.53 mL × 0.02198 g Na2CO3/mL HCl)

The number of moles of Na2CO3 present can be calculated as:

moles of Na2CO3 = mass of Na2CO3 / molar mass of Na2CO3

= 0.0337 g / 105.99 g/mol

= 0.000317 moles

Since the reaction between Na2CO3 and HCl is a 1:2 ratio, the number of moles of HCl that reacted with Na2CO3 is:

moles of HCl = 2 × moles of Na2CO3

= 2 × 0.000317 moles

= 0.000634 moles

Since the reaction between HCl and KHSO4 is a 1:1 ratio, the number of moles of KHSO4 present in the impure sample is also 0.000634 moles.

Now, we can calculate the percentage purity of the KHSO4 sample:

percentage purity = (mass of pure KHSO4 / total mass of impure sample) × 100

= (0.00317 moles × 136.17 g/mol / 1.2118 g) × 100

= 35.61%

Therefore, the percentage purity of the KHSO4 sample is 35.61%.
