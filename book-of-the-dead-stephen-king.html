19. Elaborate some impacts of the "BOOK OF THE DEAD"


Answer:

The Book of the Dead is an ancient Egyptian funerary text generally written on papyrus and used from the beginning of the New Kingdom to around 50 BCE. The original Egyptian name for the text, transliterated rw nw prt m hrw, is translated as Book of Coming Forth by Day or Book of Emerging Forth into the Light.


20. thoughts about King Lear Weeping over the Dead Body of Cordelia


Answer:

The sight of King Lear weeping over the dead body of Cordelia is a heartbreaking moment that speaks to the tragedy of the play. It is a reminder of how quickly life can be taken away and how much pain can be caused by our own mistakes. It also serves as a warning to us all about the consequences of our actions and how we should strive to make better choices in life. 23. What does the word "checkmate" mean in Persian? A. King is Dead. B. King needs Help. C. King is helpless​


Answer:

the answer is C

Explanation:

hope it helps huhu

Answer:

A. King is Dead

Explanation:

The word “checkmate” derives from the Persian phrase “Shah Met” which means “the King is Dead”.

In modern parlance, the term checkmate is a metaphor for an irrefutable and strategic victory. The term checkmate is, according to the Barnhart Etymological Dictionary, an alteration of the Farsi phrase “shāh māt” which means, literally, “the King is helpless”.

Others maintain that it means “the King is dead”, as chess reached Europe via the Islamic world, and Arabic māta means “died” or “is dead”. However, in the Pashto language (an Afghan language), the word māt still exists, meaning “destroyed, broken”.

Players would announce “Shāh” when the king was in check. “Māt” (مات) is a Persian adjective for “at a loss”, “helpless”, or “defeated”. So the king is in mate when he is ambushed, at a loss, helpless, defeated, or abandoned to his fate.
